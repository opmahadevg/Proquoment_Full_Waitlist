# Industrial Cobalt: Design System Document

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: The Industrialist’s Ledger**

Industrial Cobalt is a design system built for high-stakes B2B procurement and global logistics. It moves away from "startup soft" aesthetics into a realm of "Precision Authority." The system mimics the clarity of an industrial blueprint while maintaining the premium feel of an editorial business journal. 

The aesthetic is driven by extreme typographic scale, heavy ink-weight contrasts, and a rigid yet spacious layout that favors functional density over decorative fluff. It breaks the traditional SaaS template by using bold vertical rules, monolithic headers, and intentional "white space" that feels like a physical gallery wall.

## 2. Colors
The palette is dominated by **Electric Cobalt (#070099)** and a sophisticated range of cool-toned greys.

*   **Primary (Cobalt):** Used for authority and brand presence. It is the "ink" of the system.
*   **Surface Hierarchy:** Instead of borders, depth is created by nesting `surface-container-lowest` (pure white) cards against a `surface` (#f9f9f9) background.
*   **The "No-Line" Rule:** Sectioning is achieved through background shifts or large-scale whitespace. 1px borders are strictly limited to the global Navigation and Footer to act as "anchors" for the page content.
*   **Glassmorphism:** The top navigation utilizes a `surface/80` backdrop-blur (12px) to maintain context while scrolling through high-density data.

## 3. Typography
The system uses **Inter** exclusively but treats it as a variable tool for architectural hierarchy.

*   **Display/Hero:** 3.75rem (60px) to 5rem. Font weight is **Black (900)** with tracking at `-0.05em`. This "tight" setting creates a high-impact, headline-driven experience.
*   **Subheadings:** 1.875rem (30px) for section titles, maintaining a bold presence.
*   **The "Micro-Label":** 10px to 11px uppercase labels with wide tracking (`0.2em`). This is used for metadata, status indicators, and "eyebrow" text to provide a technical, ledger-like feel.
*   **Body:** 1.125rem (18px) for readability, ensuring that even dense "How it Works" scenarios feel accessible.

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is defined by **Tonal Stacking** rather than traditional drop shadows.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Content lives on `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff). Interactive hover states trigger a transition to `primary-container` rings or subtle upward shifts.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** While the screen uses clean planes, interactive cards employ a very soft, diffused shadow: `0px 20px 40px rgba(15, 0, 218, 0.04)`. Note the use of the primary blue hue within the shadow's color rather than neutral black.
*   **Functional Borders:** If a boundary is required for clarity (e.g., input fields), use `outline-variant` at 30% opacity.

## 5. Components
*   **Buttons:** Two distinct states. Primary buttons are pill-shaped (`rounded-full`) in solid Cobalt. Secondary/Ghost buttons use `surface-container-low` with primary-colored text, emphasizing utility over action.
*   **Cards:** Rigid containers with `1rem` (16px) corner radius. They must feature a "Scenario/Result" information architecture to maintain the "Industrialist Ledger" logic.
*   **Navigation:** A fixed-position, high-contrast bar. The logo should always be paired with a functional icon (e.g., `factory`) in a primary-colored square.
*   **Indicators:** Use emojis or Material Symbols for category identification, but keep them contained within large-scale margins to avoid clutter.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts
*   **Do:** Use extreme typographic scale (pairing 60pt headers with 10pt labels).
*   **Do:** Use the "tight" tracking on headings and "wide" tracking on labels.
*   **Don't:** Use vibrant accent colors (pinks, yellows, oranges). The system should remain strictly Cobalt, White, and Grey.
*   **Don't:** Use thin, spindly icons. Icons should have a `400` weight to match the "Ink" feel of the typography.
*   **Do:** Use 100% width for footers and headers to create a "contained" industrial feel.
